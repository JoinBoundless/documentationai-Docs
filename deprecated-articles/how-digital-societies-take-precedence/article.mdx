### **Can we find proof?**

Declarations of sovereignty must be public.

### **What constitutes a valid declaration?**

It is a unilateral, public action by a society indicating it believes it has met the 30,000-member threshold. 

### **Does the proof need to be verified by a third party?**

No.

  ## **Does the society have 30,000 members?**
  </p>

### **What counts as a member?&#xA0;**

A person issued a citizenship certificate.

### **Can a person be a member of two societies?**

No. Citizenship for the purpose of Cyber Sovereignty is singular in nature.

Since conflicted leaders cannot also take advantage of citizenship in their own society. They have the ability to join another society. They count as both members of that society and the other. 

Corporations do not count toward the citizenship numbers. 

### **How can we ensure the number was counted correctly?**

We trust digital societies to do it right until they have to prove it during the standard panel process <a href="https://agora.joinboundless.org/cyber-sovereignty/legal-concepts-of-cyber-sovereignty" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">§</a>. 

Individuals are also required to declare their citizenship when filing taxes.

### **Who defines what a "first-order effect" is?**

A first-order effect is the **direct, tangible, and immediate consequence** of an action. 

The definition should be defined in detail by the <a href="https://agora.joinboundless.org/cyber-sovereignty/the-trusted-panel" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Jurisdictional Boundary Tribunal</a>.

Obviously this would cause catastrophic harm to anyone trusting that digital society to take precedence in the future.
